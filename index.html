Instruction-Driven 3D Facial Expression Generation and Transition

Anh H. Vo1, Tae-Seok Kim1, Hulin Jin2, Soo-Mi Choi1, Yong-Guk Kim*1,
1Department of Computer Engineering, Sejong University, Seoul, Republic of Korea, 2The School of Computer Science and Technology, Anhui University, Hefei, China
Paper

Overview

Abstract

Generating facial expressions on 3D faces has been popular since it has diverse applications. This study presents a new text-guided facial expression generation framework that produces a 3D face and makes facial transitions between two arbitrary facial expressions accompanied by pose variation, starting from a 2D face image.

Initially, we introduce the Text-guided Facial Expression Decomposer (TFED) module to facilitate multimodal data learning and capture the correlation between textual descriptions and facial expression features. Subsequently, we propose the Text to Facial Expression Transition (T2FET) method, which leverages TFED and vertex reconstruction loss function to refine the semantic comprehension of latent vectors, thus generating a facial expression sequence guided by provided instruction. Lastly, we present a Facial Expression Transition model to generate smooth transitions between facial expressions.

Extensive evaluation suggests that the proposed model outperforms state-of-the-art methods on CK+ and CelebV-HQ datasets. The results show that our framework can generate facial expression trajectories according to text insinstruction. We expect that it can find various practical applications.
